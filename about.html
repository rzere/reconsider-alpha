The Shift

Most content online will be synthetic.

We can no longer assume a human was involved.

  • AI writes at scale
  • Deepfakes are trivial
  • Platforms can't verify origin
  • Regulations demand provenance & human oversight

The world is moving from trust-by-assumption → trust-by-verification.

The Problem

There is no standard way to verify: "Did a human actually create or approve this?"

Today we have:

  • AI detection: probabilistic
  • Watermarking: only for AI-generated outputs
  • KYC / accounts: identity ≠ human action
  • Digital signatures: signer ≠ human at time of creation

No one provides a cryptographic, auditable, privacy-safe proof of human origin.

How It Works

Three layers

Layer 1

Signals (Local)

  • Device attestations
  • Behavioral entropy
  • Cryptographic signing
  • No raw biometrics
Layer 2

Verification (Cloud)

  • Multi-signal fusion
  • Validation against trust anchors
  • Probability of human origin
  • Signature returned
Layer 3

Log (Public)

  • Append-only transparency ledger
  • Proofs auditable but privacy-preserving
  • Interoperable with C2PA / W3C VC

Why Now

Regulatory pressure + platform risk are forcing authenticity to become infrastructure

  • EU AI Act: Must log human oversight
  • ISO 42001: AI management systems require traceability
  • NIST AI RMF: Demand proof of accountability
  • C2PA / Provenance: Covers synthetic output, not human proof
  • Platforms: Need a neutral verification layer

Reconsider fills the final missing piece of AI governance: "Was a human in the loop?"
